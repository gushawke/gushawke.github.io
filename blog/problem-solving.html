Problem Solving

Got blocked on a simple problem


I have found JavaScript very challenging and have been stumped on simple problems more often than not! I guess learning a new language can be like this at first, however if I was to compare my knowledge to 2 weeks ago I have learnt a lot and certainly am familiar with functions, objects and variables now. When I was blocked in sprint 4 on the FizzBuzz challenge, I was in the weeds for hours. This was not a nice feeling especially with time pressures. I found that taking some time away from the computer helped with this feeling, and gave me a clearer head when I was filtering through material to find the solution.



Solved a problem in an elegant way.


I wouldn’t say I have the confidence yet to solve anything in an elegant way! However there were moments where I shouted “Yes!!” particularly within the Gradebook segment. This was a tough challenge for me and I spent a lot of time researching how to find the average of an array. This was much easier said than done, and I did need to use the solution as a guide in the end. However I found a similar problem on the Udemy online course which I started before Dev Academy and this helped my learning. After I had solved this my confidence was boosted and it felt good not going to lie! This reflection exercise certainly helped my learning and proved practice is everything!!

Problem Solving Techniques


Pseudocode

This technique of writing out the problem in plain english like a baking recipe again can be easier said than done. The instructions on the handbook helped, however when it came time for me to write things down I was clueless at times where to start. This will come easier with time and a useful tool to implement.


Trying something

Trial and error - I performed a lot of this. However at times I was way off and can be a fast way to get frustrated. This should be left for when you have done some research on the problem and when you’re confident on a particular challenge.


Rubber ducky method

Talking to a rubber duck instead of interrupting someone else would be my preference. I don’t like asking for help and sometimes feel bad even asking Jo$eph for some guidance…


Reading error messages

Sometimes these were helpful for when there is an unnecessary symbol or undefined variable etc. I got a lot of error messages….


Console.logging

This wasn’t really a technique I used on Repl.it however useful for breaking down problems.


Googling

Google and MDN are very useful resources for guidance.


Asking your peers for help

As I am not sure who anyone is - I have not yet asked peers.


Asking coaches for help

Jo$eph has been a great help! Thanks bro


Improving your process with reflection

As mentioned above, reflecting on functions with the Udemy online course helped deepen my understanding. Also reflecting at the end of the day helps cement learnings.
