Neuroplasticity

Understanding neuroplasticity can improve your performance of the brain, should you decide to implement neuroplasticity principles. Following these principles can lead to optimum results, which can give people the motivation to keep repeating a new task that doesn’t come natural to them. I will aim to engage these principles when I generally would like to improve at something in particular. One of the principles is Salience, which suggests that the training experience must be meaningful to the person in order to cause change (plasticity). Once I have given myself the commitment to improve at something, these principles will guide me on my journey to improvement.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?


Ways that can increase your neuroplasticity are picking up a new instrument, testing your memory, exercising to give the much needed blood flow to provide oxygen to the neurons, visualising the experience, paying attention, meditation to focus the mind, and arguably the most effective - repetition.

Growth Mindset


Discuss what it is and why it is relevant A growth mindset is relevant if YOU want to remain relevant. Otherwise embracing the same old habits and routines our mind and neurological pathways become weaker as we age. In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you? I inadvertently realised that I have always embraced change throughout my life and thrive when I am experiencing new things or getting better at new things. What was a surprise was I didn’t realise that what made me feel good would have been the growth mindset I was embracing, rather I thought it was the physical exercise itself which made me feel alive. I now believe that it would be the growth I was experiencing that made me feel alive.
