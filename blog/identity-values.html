Identity & Values

Mō tātou, ā, mō kā uri ā muri ake nei
– for us and our children after us

Using the evidence gathered, describe your core personal values.

I would like to think that I am an ethical consumer, or at least try to be. I believe we all need to make small changes to our lifestyles and diet to help reduce the impact we humans are having on this planet, and preserve this delicate place for generations after us.

I am faced with moral challenges daily, as our system is built on cheap imports such as plastics that end up in our oceans. Our supermarkets are full of plastics which will take thousands of years to decompose. And a lot of the time we don't have a choice.

Kaitiakitanga
is a value which resonates with me and the the whakatauki above. This is roughly translated into stewardship for people, environment, knowledge and culture.

Manaakitanga
pay respect to each other.

Rangatiratanga
maintain a high degree of personal integrity and ethical behaviour in all actions and decisions we undertake.

I believe my cultural identity has shaped this belief, and has resulted in change in the way I now consume. For most of my life I didn’t realise the impact I was personally having on the environment around me, and simple everyday choices can go a long way in fighting climate change, by increasing demand for more sustainable options. I.e. locally made goods reduce the demand for international shipping and mass production. Choosing vegetarian food increases demand for plant based alternatives (and they are getting pretty good!).


Using the evidence gathered, describe your strengths and limitations.

My strengths in terms of learning are linked to curiosity. When I am truly interested in a topic I generally gain a deep understanding, which usually involves research and books. My weaknesses would be when I am not interested in a topic, I find the learning punishing. (I don’t anticipate Dev Academy being at all punishing as although CSS is rather dry, I know it’s just a part of the process of getting from A to B). In terms of career pathway, my interests should guide me toward what sparks my curiosity, and I’ve been in a career up until now where I stopped learning - more just learning from experience over time. Hence learning something which I am curious or passionate about should lead lay down a solid foundation for my career.

A time where I have encountered resistance or tension whilst trying to work productively within a team recently was when Auckland went into lockdown and we were working remotely. Our team lost it’s glue early on, and impacted productivity and ultimately motivation. (There were some other issues however let’s not go there!).

Strategies I would use to keep on task and productive, were improving my workspace at home. This allowed me to treat my workspace like an office, opposed to the couch or bedroom. This also gave me a routine each morning to “go to work”. Although we would have a meeting each morning and catch up, people were disengaged and I was disengaged. We wouldn’t get the same small chat interactions as you would in the office.

Another strategy I would do is cal my collegues up for non-work related catch ups. This helped my engagement and improved my work. If I was to work remotely again I would try implementing more initiatives to boost morale and engagement, such as some kind of online game or social catch up.

I believe remote working on top of lockdown is very challenging on many aspects including relationships and mental health. Especially if I am in a leadership position, I would not make targets the focus, rather focus on comaradary, which I believe would improve targets ultimately.
